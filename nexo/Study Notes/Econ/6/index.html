The Business Cycle

Fluctuations in GDP around the natural growth rate. The economy moves through phases of expansion and contraction.

🎯 Potential Output vs Actual Output

📈 Potential Output
  • Maximum sustainable output at full employment
  • Grows at the natural growth rate
  • Represents long-run trend
📉 Actual Output
  • Real GDP observed in the economy
  • Fluctuates around potential output
  • Above = inflationary pressure, Below = recessionary gap

Macroeconomic Equilibrium

Equilibrium occurs when aggregate demand equals aggregate supply — no tendency for change.

🏡 The Four Sectors

🏠
Household
Consumers, suppliers of labour
🏭
Business
Firms producing goods & services
🏛️
Government
Public spending & taxation
🌍
Foreign
International trade & capital flows

📊 Three Approaches to Equilibrium

AD = AS
Y = E    (Output = Aggregate Expenditure)
W = J    (Withdrawals = Injections)

Aggregate Demand (AD)

The total quantity of goods and services demanded in the economy at a given price level.

Aggregate Supply (AS)

The total quantity of goods and services that firms are willing to produce at a given price level.

💰

Aggregate Expenditure Components

Total spending in the economy is the sum of four components:

AE = C + I + G + (X − M)
🛍️
Consumption (C)
Household spending on goods & services
🏭
Investment (I)
Business spending on capital goods
🏛️
Government (G)
Government expenditure on goods & services
🌍
Net Exports (X−M)
Exports minus Imports
📈

The Consumption Function

The relationship between disposable income and consumption spending.

C = a + bYd
💵
a = Autonomous Consumption
Consumption when income is zero → C = a (dissaving)
📈
b = MPC
Marginal Propensity to Consume = ΔC / ΔY

📋 Marginal Propensity to Consume (MPC)

MPC = ΔC / ΔY

The fraction of an additional unit of income that is spent on consumption. 0 < MPC < 1

💰 High MPC
  • Consumers spend most of additional income
  • Large multiplier effect
  • Often in developing economies
💱 Low MPC
  • Consumers save most of additional income
  • Small multiplier effect
  • Often in developed economies
💱

The Savings Function

Savings is the part of income not consumed.

S = Y − C

Deriving the savings function from the consumption function:

S = −a + (1−b)Y

📋 MPS — Marginal Propensity to Save

MPS = 1 − MPC

The fraction of additional income that is saved. MPC + MPS = 1

💵
Autonomous Savings
−a — dissaving at zero income
📈
Induced Savings
(1−b)Y — savings that depend on income
🏠

Closed Economy Equilibrium

A closed economy has no foreign sector. Equilibrium is achieved when:

Y = C + I + G
S + T − R = I + G   (W = J approach)

📋 Worked Example

Given: C = 100 + 0.75Yd,   I = 50,   G = 200

In equilibrium: Y = C + I + G
Y = 100 + 0.75Y + 50 + 200
Y − 0.75Y = 350
0.25Y = 350
Y = 1400
🌍

Open Economy Equilibrium

An open economy includes international trade. Equilibrium condition:

Y = C + I + G + (X − M)

📋 Worked Example

Given: C = 200 + 0.8Yd,   I = 100,   G = 200,   Net Exports = −100

In equilibrium: Y = C + I + G + (X − M)
Y = 200 + 0.8Y + 100 + 200 + (−100)
Y − 0.8Y = 400
0.2Y = 400
Y = 2000

📈 Effect of an Increase in Investment

If investment increases by ΔI, the equilibrium output changes by the multiplier times the change in investment.

ΔY = k × ΔI
⚖️

Closed vs Open Economy

🏠 Closed Economy
  • No international trade
  • Y = C + I + G
  • W = S + T − R
  • J = I + G
  • Unaffected by global shocks
🌍 Open Economy
  • Includes exports & imports
  • Y = C + I + G + (X−M)
  • W = S + T − R + M
  • J = I + G + X
  • Exposed to global economic conditions
💰

The Multiplier

The multiplier measures the magnified effect of a change in autonomous expenditure on equilibrium output.

k = 1 / (1 − MPC) = 1 / MPS
ΔY = k × ΔE

📋 Examples

📈
MPC = 0.8
k = 1 / (1 − 0.8) = 5
📈
MPC = 0.9
k = 1 / (1 − 0.9) = 10
📈
MPC = 0.75
k = 1 / (1 − 0.75) = 4
