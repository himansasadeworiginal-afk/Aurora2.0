What is Economics?

Every subject in the world can be divided into two main parts:

🔬 Natural Sciences
  • Studies natural phenomena
  • Based on laboratory tests
  • Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Geology, Space Science, Medical Science
👥 Social Sciences
  • Studies behavior of people in society
  • Political Science, Psychology, Criminology, Sociology, Geography, History
  • Economics — studies economic behavior of people
👑
Economics is the Queen of Social Sciences
Adam Smith — Father of Economics, author of The Wealth of Nations (1776)
📋

Microeconomics vs Macroeconomics

🧠 Microeconomics
  • Study of economics as units
  • Small units: households, firms, individuals
  • Analyzes separate economic variables
🌍 Macroeconomics
  • Study of economics as a whole
  • Aggregate economic behavior
  • National income, inflation, unemployment
⚖️

Positive & Normative Statements

✅ Positive Statements
  • Answer: what is, what happened, what will happen
  • Can be verified with real-world facts
  • Scientific and objective
  • Ex: "Positive relationship exists between money supply and price level"
💭 Normative Statements
  • Answer: what should be / should happen
  • Based on personal views, beliefs, traditions
  • Subjective — hard to test objectively
  • Ex: "Sri Lanka should obtain foreign aids for development"
🛒

Needs & Wants

❤️ Needs
  • Basic necessities to sustain life
  • Combined with biological sources
  • Limited
  • Do not change with time
  • Common to all
  • Ex: Food
✨ Wants
  • Various forms of fulfilling needs
  • Combined with traditions, values, culture
  • Unlimited
  • Change with time
  • Differ from person to person
  • Ex: Rice, Bread, String Hoppers, Hoppers

Economic vs Non-Economic Goods

💰 Economic Goods
  • Scarce supply
  • Resource cost involved
  • Opportunity cost exists
  • Has ownership
  • Involves a price
🌊 Non-Economic Goods
  • Unlimited supply at zero price
  • No resource cost
  • No opportunity cost
  • No ownership

Can non-economic goods become economic goods? Yes! Examples: oxygen for divers, bottled water, solar panels.

Scarcity & Choice

⚠️
Scarcity
Limited resources × Unlimited wants → the central economic problem

Scarcity vs Poverty

⚠️ Scarcity
  • Lack of resources relative to wants
  • Cannot be reduced by government policy
💰 Poverty
  • Total absence of resources for basic needs
  • Can be reduced by government policy

Scarcity vs Shortage

⚠️ Scarcity
  • Permanent resource limitation
  • Central problem of every economy
⏰ Shortage
  • Delay of stocks; demand > supply
  • Temporary — disappears with arrival of stocks

Three Basic Economic Questions

📋 What to produce — in what quantity?
Due to scarcity and alternative uses
⚙️ How to produce?
Labour-intensive vs Capital-intensive techniques
👥 For whom to produce?
Distribution of limited goods and services
💱

Opportunity Cost

💱
Opportunity Cost
The value of the next best alternative forgone when making a choice
Opportunity Cost = Value of Next Best Alternative Forgone
✅
Positive Concept
Only with economic goods
💰
Real, not Financial
Shows real value, not monetary
🧑‍💬
Individual
Expressed only by the person choosing

When can opportunity cost be zero?

  • Unlimited resources (non-economic goods)
  • Absence of alternative uses
  • Unemployed resources

🏭
Market Economy

🏰

Mixed Economy

Traditional Economy

Economic Growth & Depression

📈
Economic Growth
PPF shifts right — increased production capacity
📉
Economic Depression
PPF shifts left — decreased production capacity

Factors Affecting Production Capacity

↗️ Increase (Growth)
  • Increase in resources (Land, Labour, Capital, Entrepreneurship)
  • Increase in productivity (Investment, Technology, Management)
  • Increase in innovations
  • Economic development
↘️ Decrease (Depression)
  • Decrease in resources
  • Decrease in productivity
  • Decrease in innovations
  • Natural disasters / Civil war
