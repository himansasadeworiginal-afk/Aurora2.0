★ What is Entrepreneurship?

“Entrepreneurship is the process of extracting environmental opportunities creatively and generating innovation while bearing risks.”
  • William D. Bygrave: “An entrepreneur is someone who perceives an opportunity and creates an organization to pursue it.”
  • Joseph Schumpeter: “An entrepreneur is the person who destroys the existing economic order by introducing new products and services.”
  • Oxford Dictionary: “Entrepreneurship is the bridge between capital and labor.”
  • Richard Nantinut: “Entrepreneurship is facing up to uncertainties.”
  • ILO: “Entrepreneurship is the ability to launch a new company and carry it on successfully.”

⚠ Importance of Entrepreneurship

  • Enormous need for entrepreneurs who can find innovations and react to rapid changes
  • Need for generating innovations to survive in the competitive business world
  • Entrepreneurs give leadership to the changing world
  • Innovations uplift living standards through satisfying human needs
  • Developing business functions in line with changing technology
  • Creating employment through starting new businesses

All enterprises are businesses, whereas all businesses are not enterprises. The result of entrepreneurship is known as business.

☰ Benefits of Entrepreneurship

Individual Benefits

  • Make use of individual abilities
  • Higher benefits through higher commitments
  • Social status and acceptance
  • Self-satisfaction
  • Personal and financial gains

Institutional Benefits

  • Easy to face competition
  • Expansion of business activities
  • Continuous development
  • Expansion of market shares
  • Becoming industry expert

Social & Economic Benefits

  • Creation of new employment
  • New goods and services for consumers
  • Developing standard of living
  • Using regional resources
  • Development of new markets
  • Creation of income opportunities
  • Increase in national production through innovations
  • Increase in employment level
  • Faster economic development
  • Increase in production → employment → income → purchasing power → demand → business expansion → economic development

⚖ Trends in Entrepreneurship

Global Entrepreneurship

  • Engaging in trade globally using technology
  • Working beyond borders
  • Understanding other cultures

Social Entrepreneurship

  • Innovations to solve social problems
  • Intention of social service
  • Mental satisfaction, acceptance, praise

Green Entrepreneurship

  • Activities with positive environmental impact
  • Protecting environment as core concept
  • Sensitive to environmental issues

Intra Entrepreneurship

  • Creative people within organizations
  • Changes in products and production methods
  • Introduced and implemented within the institute

⚗ Entrepreneurial Process

  1. Discovery — Generate ideas, identify opportunities through environmental study, study the marketplace
  2. Concept Development & Business Plan — Prepare a detailed program clarifying the business idea
  3. Resourcing — Identify and acquire financial, human and physical resources
  4. Actualization — Carry out operational activities, use resources to fulfil aims
  5. Harvesting — Take decisions regarding future growth and development

☶ The Entrepreneur

“The person who sees changes in the marketplace as opportunities for businesses is an entrepreneur. His armory is product innovation.”
— Peter F. Drucker
AspectEntrepreneurBusinessman
OrientationInnovation & opportunityProfit & operations
RiskHigh risk takerCalculated risk
ApproachCreative, disruptiveSystematic, established
FocusChange & growthStability & efficiency
  • Willingness to take risks — Faces risks rather than avoids them
  • Creativity — Ability to see, think and take actions differently
  • Commitment — Works with great enthusiasm until objectives achieved
  • Self-confidence — Believes he can successfully overcome problems
  • Self-reliant — Puts own ideas into effect rather than depending on others
  • Leadership skills — Leads followers by encouraging them
  • Interpersonal skills — Maintains favorable relationships
  • Communication skills — Maintains proper coordination
  • Basic management skills — Planning, organizing, leading, controlling

Ways to develop: Self-evaluation, Training, Education, Profiting from experiences

⚙ Small & Medium Scale Businesses

  • Having a small market
  • Invested capital being low
  • Fewer staff employed
  • Family labor used most probably
  • Decision making based on personal information
  • Owner is the chief manager
  • Targeted at local markets
  • Fewer financial bonds
  • Modern technology being used
  • Labor intensive → more employment
  • Contribution to national product growth
  • Utilizing inland resources
  • Minimizing urbanization problems through regional development
  • Minimizing income inequality
  • Supplementing raw materials and finished goods for large-scale businesses
  • Engaging in affairs not performed by large businesses

Reasons for Success

  • Sufficient infrastructure
  • Financial and tax incentives
  • Expanded market
  • Institutional and marketing facilities
  • Sound management
  • Enthusiasm and commitment

Reasons for Failure

  • Financial problems
  • Inability to face competition
  • Weak management
  • Less entrepreneurial skills
  • Insufficient infrastructure
  • Low technology awareness
  • Studying and evaluating one’s own abilities and skills
  • Market availability and entry possibilities
  • Opening capital and working capital requirements
  • Type of business organization
  • Existing rules and regulations
  • Existing competition
