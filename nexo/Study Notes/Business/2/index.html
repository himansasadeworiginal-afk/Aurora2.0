Social Responsibility

The obligation of a business to perform its activities in an unbiased way and act favorably toward all stakeholders — social responsibility goes beyond legal obligations to include ethical and discretionary expectations.

Key Definitions

🌍
World Business Council
"The commitment of a business to contribute to sustainable economic development, working with employees, their families, and the local community."
🧬
Mallen Baker
"Companies treat stakeholders ethically & responsibly beyond legal requirements."
📚
Narayan Moorthy
"Social responsibility is the intelligent & objective concern for the welfare of society that restrains individual/group behavior from destructive activities."

Sustainable Development

Sustainable development is development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

"When the last tree is cut, the last river poisoned, and the last fish dead, we will discover that we cannot eat money." — Seattle, Red Indian Leader

💰 Economic

4R Concept for Sustainability

The 4R framework guides businesses in minimizing environmental impact through waste management and resource conservation.

♻️ Recycle
  • Waste → new materials
  • Upcycle: hay → paper
  • Low cycle: vehicles → iron
  • Reduces landfill waste
🔄 Reuse
  • Using products repeatedly
  • Lunch boxes instead of sheets
  • Refillable containers
  • Extends product lifecycle
➖ Reduce
  • Minimizing waste generation
  • No non-degradable polythene
  • No plastic bottles
  • Lower resource consumption
🔄 Replace
  • Replacing consumed resources
  • Reforestation
  • Fish farming
  • Renewable resource regeneration
⚠️

Business Ethics & Code of Ethics

Business ethics refers to acting favorably toward all parties by acknowledging what is good and bad in business conduct. It provides a moral framework for decision-making.

Examples of Business Ethics

  • Quality Goods Building trust through consistent product standards
  • Ethical Accounting Transparent financial reporting prevents misconduct
  • Fair HR Management Equitable hiring, promotion, and compensation

Key Factors for a Code of Ethics

🏭 Nature of Business 🧍 Customers 🧑‍💼 Employees 🤛 Competition 🌍 Socio-Culture 🏢 Organizational Culture ✅ Practicableness
