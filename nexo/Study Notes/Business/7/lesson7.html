⚠ Risk & Insurance

The space of occurring damage which can be estimated financially is known as a risk in insurance.

Business risks include: fire, theft, business loss, damage to properties.

Insurable Risks

  • Can be forecast
  • Calculable in money terms
  • Not certain (random)
  • No connection to other insured loss
  • Verifiable cause, place, time

E.g. fire, motor accidents, money in transit, debtor non-payment

Non-Insurable Risks

  • Business losses
  • Natural reasons (depreciation, expiration, vaporization)
  • One’s ability/strength (exam failure, loss of love)
  • Design changes
Insurance is an agreement between the insurer and the insured whereby the insured pays the premium and the insurer pays compensation for insured risks.
  • Insured (1st party) — Person who presents proposal / obtains coverage
  • Insurer (2nd party) — Insurance company that accepts the proposal
  • Third party — External party that comes into activation of the agreement

Insurance agreement validity requires: idea of legal obligation, insurance offer/proposal, acceptance, legal validity, ability to have legal obligation by parties.

Insurance policy is a written certificate given by the insurance institute to the insured after the agreement.

♺ Principles of Insurance

  • Insurable Interest — Economic advantage from existence; disadvantage from loss. E.g. spouse has interest in each other’s life; legal owner in property; creditor in debtor’s life
  • Utmost Good Faith — Both parties reveal all information related to the agreement morally
  • Indemnity — Payment of adequate compensation to restore the damaged property (e.g. Rs. 2.5L paid for Rs. 2.5L damage on Rs. 40L vehicle)
  • Contribution — When insured in multiple companies, they pay proportionately (e.g. 5:3:2 ratio)
  • Subrogation — After settling loss, insurer obtains other rights/advantages from external parties (e.g. insurer takes debris after total loss)
  • Proximate Cause — Compensation paid only if the proximate (nearest) reason is covered by the policy
  • Insurable interest, utmost good faith, and proximate cause apply to life insurance
  • Indemnity, contribution, subrogation do not apply to life insurance (loss is immeasurable)
  • Reinsurance — Insurer insures a risk it has undertaken under another insurance company
  • Underwriting — Dividing a high-value risk among several insurance companies

☷ Life Insurance / Long Term Insurance

Provides monetary benefits to the insured or beneficiaries against risks due to demise, disability, or old age.

If the insured dies during the period, the agreed value goes to beneficiaries. If they live until the end, they obtain the policy value and benefits.

🔥 Fire Insurance

Covers losses caused to a person, building, or property due to fire.
  • Losses by riots / strikes
  • Losses by explosions
  • Losses by electricity
  • Earthquakes / tsunami
  • Flood / whirl winds / storms
  • Aircraft crashes
  • Losses from crashes
  • Consequential losses

🔒 Theft & Marine Insurance

Covers risks to business premises or homes from thievery. Natural disaster insurance covers losses from storms etc.

Covers losses to ships and goods while transporting by sea. Two parts:

Hull Insurance

  • Full option (comprehensive) policy
  • Total loss policy
  • Port risk policy
  • Maintenance policy
  • Construction all risk policy

Cargo Insurance

  • Valued policy
  • Open cover / floating policy
  • Future time policy

⚖ Liability Insurance

Ensuring the liability for losses caused to other parties is known as liability insurance.
  • General Liability Insurance — For unexpected losses from properties or business activities
  • Product Liability Insurance — For losses to consumers from consuming products sold
  • Employer Liability Insurance — Covers employer’s compensation liability for employee accidents at work
  • Goods in Transit Insurance — Covers losses to goods from loading to unloading during land transport
  • Money in Transit Insurance — Covers loss while taking money from one place to another

🚚 Goods & Money in Transit

Goods in Transit Insurance

  • Covers losses from loading to unloading
  • For transport on land
  • Protects goods during the journey

Money in Transit Insurance

  • Covers loss while moving money
  • Between locations
  • Essential for cash-heavy businesses
