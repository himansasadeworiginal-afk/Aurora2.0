Fiscal Policy

Functions related to government revenue and expenditure.

Government Revenue

Govt Revenue = Tax Revenue + Non-tax Revenue

Types of Taxes

💵
Income Tax
Corporation tax, PAYE tax on earnings
💲
Value Added Tax (VAT)
On wholesale/retail transactions & exports
🍺
Excise Duties
On liquor, tobacco, & similar goods
🚚
Customs Duties
On imported goods
📊
Other Taxes
Development tax, Cess, Telecom tax

Direct vs Indirect Tax

💰 Direct Tax
  • Paid by the liable party directly
  • Cannot be transferred to another
  • Ex: Income tax, Corporation tax
🔄 Indirect Tax
  • Can be transferred to another party
  • Passed on through prices
  • Ex: VAT, Excise duties, Customs duties

Government Expenditure

🔄 Recurrent Expenditure
  • Salaries & wages
  • Interest payments
  • Ongoing operational costs
🏗️ Capital Expenditure
  • Roads & bridges
  • Infrastructure projects
  • Long-term asset creation

Impact of Fiscal Policy on Businesses

  • Customs duties protect local industries from foreign competition
  • Taxes on inland products raise prices of goods
  • Government spending on infrastructure simplifies business operations
🏦

Monetary Policy

Policy to control money supply in the economy. Executed by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka.

Main aim: Price Stability Inflation Control

Key Instruments of Monetary Policy

1. 💵 Interest Rate (Standing Facility Rates)

SDFR
  • Standing Deposit Facility Rate
  • Paid when banks invest surplus in govt securities
SLFR
  • Standing Lending Facility Rate
  • Charged when banks borrow pledging securities
Bank Interest Rate
  • Final loan provider rate to customers

2. 📋 Statutory Reserve Requirements (SRR)

Deposits banks must maintain at the central bank.

  • ↓ SRR → ↑ Money Supply
  • ↑ SRR → ↓ Money Supply

3. 💱 Open Market Operations (OMO)

Buying and selling government securities.

  • Central bank buys securities → ↑ Money Supply
  • Central bank sells securities → ↓ Money Supply

4. 🔄 Changing Discount Rate

Controlling money supply through discount rate changes.

5. 🎯 Imposing Credit Limits

📊 Quantitative
  • Controlling total credit volume
  • Asset/capital ratios
  • Minimum deposits for LCs
🎯 Qualitative
  • Controlling loan direction
  • Motivating priority sectors
  • Limiting consumption loans

6. 📅 Managing Inactive Loans

Direct strategy to control existing loan portfolios.

Impact of Monetary Policy on Businesses

↓ Interest Rate → ↓ Commercial Bank Rates → ↑ Loans → ↑ Investment → ↑ Production → ↓ Prices → ↑ Sales → ↑ Profit
⚖️

Fiscal Policy vs Monetary Policy

💰 Fiscal Policy
  • Government revenue & expenditure
  • Managed by the government (Treasury)
  • Uses taxation & spending
  • Impacts budget deficits/surpluses
🏦 Monetary Policy
  • Control of money supply
  • Managed by Central Bank
  • Uses interest rates, SRR, OMO
  • Impacts inflation & credit availability
