☂ What is Money?

Anything that is generally accepted in exchange for goods and services is considered as money.

Before money, the barter system was used — direct exchange of goods — which had many difficulties. Money emerged as a common medium of exchange acceptable to all.

⚙ Functions of Money

  • Medium of exchange — Intermediary in exchange of goods/services (e.g. buying a phone for Rs. 30,000)
  • Standard of value — Measuring the value of goods and services (e.g. price of shoes Rs. 1,999.90)
  • Store of value — Can be stored for future use without getting outdated (e.g. saving Rs. 10,000)
  • Deferred payment — Agreement for future payment (e.g. buy goods in Jan, pay in March)
  • Having general recognition
  • Durability
  • Difficulty in imitation
  • Easy movability
  • Easy identifiability / homogeneity
  • Ability to divide into small units (divisibility)

♺ Categories of Money

Currency

  • Notes & coins issued on orders of financial authority
  • 100% liquidity

Bank Money

  • Balances in demand/current accounts
  • Payments made via cheques
  • High liquidity (not 100%)

Near Money

  • Assets that function as a store of value
  • Can be converted to cash easily
  • Time deposits, savings deposits, Treasury bills

E-Money

  • Money from digital technology
  • Used in inland & international transactions
  • Debit cards, credit cards, pre-paid cards
  • Automatic Teller Machine (ATM)
  • Automatic Deposits Machine (ADM)
  • Cheques Deposits Machines (CDM)
  • Electronic Funds Transfer point of sales
  • Telebanking, Internet banking, Television banking

⚗ Central Bank of Sri Lanka

Established on 28th August 1950 under the Monetary Act No. 58 of 1949. Founder Governor: Mr. John Exter.

Two Main Objectives

  • Maintain economic and price stability — Protect domestic currency value, avoid inflation/deflation
  • Maintain stability of the financial system — Create security, identify and minimize threats
  • Manipulation of finance policies
  • Manipulation of exchange ratio policies
  • Management of official foreign reserves
  • Issuing and distribution of currency
  • Supervisor of banks and non-banking institutions
  • Financial agent, banker and economic advisor of the government
  • Settlement accounts for commercial banks and primary traders
  • SLIPS — Inter-bank payment system (now Lanka Clear Pvt Ltd, brand: LankaPay)
  • SWIFT — Universal Interbank Financial Electronic Communication Network
  • RTGS — Real Time Gross Settlement for high-value transfers
  • SSDS (LankaSecure) — Paperless securities depository system
  • Lanka Settle System — RTGS + SSDS combined

✎ Cheques

A cheque is a written order issued by a bank current account holder (drawer) to the commercial bank (drawee) to pay the amount stated to the person named (payee) or bearer.

Bearer Cheque

  • "Or Bearer" not cancelled
  • Can be transferred by handing over
  • Like using currency notes

Order Cheque

  • "Or Bearer" struck off, "Order" written
  • Requires endorsement to transfer
  • Higher safety level

Crossing (drawing two parallel lines on the cheque) adds safety.

  • General Crossing — Two parallel lines with or without statements
  • Special Crossing — Name of a commercial bank stated; must be deposited in that bank
  • "Not Negotiable" — Transferee has no greater right than transferor
  • "Account Payee Only" — Must be deposited in payee’s account

Dishonoring occurs when the bank rejects payment.

  • Insufficient funds
  • Drawer stops payment (countermanded)
  • Drawer bankrupt or deceased
  • Garnishee order (court order)
  • Errors in writing, account closed

♻ Electronic Money

The electronic exchange of cash or cash exchanged through a computer network is known as e-money.
TypeDescription
Credit CardBuy goods/services up to a limit; interest-free loan period; cash from ATM
Debit CardUse money from account; no interest; ATM withdrawals
Pre-Paid CardPay in advance; no account needed; e.g. phone cards, travel passes
Smart CardEmbedded chip with money value; reloadable; acts as e-purse

Advantages

  • Minimized transaction costs
  • Safety of cash
  • 24/7 transactions
  • Convenient payments
  • Various benefits & discounts
  • Overseas usability

Disadvantages

  • Interest, penalties on late payments
  • Fraud risk
  • Addiction to unnecessary consumption
  • Technical interruptions
  • Lack of knowledge for some users
