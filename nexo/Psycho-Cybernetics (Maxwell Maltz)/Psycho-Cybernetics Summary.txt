PSYCHO-CYBERNETICS
By Maxwell Maltz, M.D. (Updated and Expanded Edition)

============================================================
SUMMARY
============================================================

ORIGIN STORY: THE PLASTIC SURGEON'S DISCOVERY
----------------------------------------------
Dr. Maxwell Maltz was a plastic surgeon who noticed something remarkable: when he changed a patient's face, he often changed their personality, confidence, and even abilities. But he also noticed a troubling pattern -- some patients whose faces were "fixed" still felt ugly and insecure inside.

This led to his breakthrough insight: these people weren't suffering from a physical problem. They were suffering from a distorted self-image -- an inaccurate mental picture of themselves that no scalpel could reach.

After studying cybernetics (the science of automatic guidance systems in machines), Maltz realized the human brain operates like a built-in servo-mechanism -- a goal-striving device that steers toward whatever target you give it. He called this system the "Success Mechanism."

Psycho-Cybernetics was born -- the science of using your built-in success mechanism to achieve any goal you set for yourself.

CORE CONCEPT: THE SELF-IMAGE
-----------------------------
The most important psychological discovery of the 20th century, according to Maltz, is the self-image.

The self-image is your own conception of "the sort of person I am." It has been built from:
- Past experiences, successes and failures
- Humiliations and triumphs
- How other people reacted to you (especially in early childhood)

Two critical laws govern the self-image:
1. All your actions, feelings, behaviors -- even your abilities -- are always consistent with this self-image. You will "act like" the sort of person you conceive yourself to be. You literally cannot act otherwise, despite all conscious effort or willpower.
2. You cannot outperform your self-image. If you see yourself as a failure, you'll find ways to fail even if opportunity is dumped in your lap.

The good news: the self-image can be changed. And when it is, your entire life changes.

THE SUCCESS MECHANISM
---------------------
Your brain and nervous system form a built-in guidance system -- a servo-mechanism -- that operates automatically to help you achieve your goals. It works like:
- A guided missile that corrects its course by making a series of mistakes
- A thermostat that automatically adjusts temperature
- A computer that processes data and produces results

This Success Mechanism can help you:
- Get answers to problems
- Invent and create
- Run a business
- Sell merchandise
- Develop a better personality
- Attain peace of mind

It works automatically -- but ONLY on the data you feed it. Garbage in, garbage out. If you feed it images of failure, it will produce failure. Feed it images of success, and it will produce success.

IMAGINATION: THE FIRST KEY
---------------------------
Imagination sets the goal "picture" that your automatic mechanism works on. You act -- or fail to act -- not because of will, but because of imagination.

Key truth: Your brain and nervous system cannot tell the difference between a real experience and one that is vividly imagined.

Athletes who practice mentally (visualizing perfect performance) improve almost as much as those who practice physically. This is because the same neural pathways are being strengthened.

Creative Imagination vs. Destructive Imagination:
- Creative: You imagine success, possibilities, solutions. Your mechanism works toward this target.
- Destructive: You imagine failure, worst-case scenarios, obstacles. Your mechanism works toward this target too.

You're always using imagination. The question is: constructively or destructively?

DEHYPNOTIZE YOURSELF FROM FALSE BELIEFS
-----------------------------------------
False beliefs are self-hypnosis. When a teacher told young Alfred Adler he was "dumb in math," Adler accepted the belief -- and his grades proved it. Only when he broke free from that false belief did his actual ability emerge.

Common false beliefs that hold people back:
- "I'm not the type who..."
- "I've always been bad at..."
- "That's just the way I am"
- "I don't look like a successful person"
- "I'm too old to change"

These beliefs were formed in the past -- often in childhood -- and accepted uncritically. They are not facts. They are hypotheses that you can test, challenge, and replace.

THE HABIT OF HAPPINESS
-----------------------
Happiness is native to the human mind. It is not something you earn or deserve. It is not a reward for suffering. Happiness is a HABIT that can be acquired.

"Be happy -- and you will be good, more successful, healthier, and feel more charitably toward others."

Dr. Schindler defined happiness as "a state of mind in which our thinking is pleasant a good share of the time." From a medical standpoint:
- When happy, you see better, hear better, taste better
- Eyesight improves immediately with pleasant thoughts
- Memory is greatly improved
- All internal organs function better
- You perform better in every domain

Misconceptions about happiness:
- It's NOT earned or deserved
- It's NOT a reward for suffering
- It's NOT something that happens when everything goes right
- It IS a habit, an attitude, a way of approaching life

RELAXATION AND THE SUCCESS MECHANISM
--------------------------------------
Modern man tries to solve everything by conscious thought ("forebrain thinking"). But the forebrain is the operator of the machine, not the machine itself. It poses problems -- it doesn't solve them.

The Success Mechanism works best when you're relaxed. Stress, anxiety, and excessive effort jam the mechanism.

"Gospel of Relaxation" -- William James said modern man was too tense, too concerned for results, too anxious. The solution: form the habit of freeing your thoughts from egoistic preoccupation about results.

Key principle: Decide on your goal consciously, then TRUST your mechanism to find the way. Don't micromanage the process.

THE 8 INGREDIENTS OF SUCCESS (S-U-C-C-E-S-S)
============================================================

S - SENSE OF DIRECTION
You must have a goal to shoot for. The creative mechanism is goal-striving. Without a clear target, it has nothing to work on. Many people want to "improve" but have no clear idea of what improvement looks like.

U - UNDERSTANDING
See things as they are, not as you fear they are. Use reason to examine your beliefs. Don't jump to conclusions. Understand that mistakes are part of the process -- not evidence of personal deficiency.

C - COURAGE
Act in spite of fear. Courage is not the absence of fear -- it's acting with purpose despite fear. The only way to build courage is to practice it. Take one step, and the next becomes easier.

C - COMPASSION
Understanding others' struggles builds connection. A successful personality is not cold or ruthless -- it's warm, empathetic, and able to relate to others genuinely.

E - ESTEEM (SELF-ESTEEM)
Use self-esteem for yourself, not against yourself. Big personalities make mistakes and admit them. Only little people are afraid to be wrong. Your worth is not contingent on being perfect.

S - SELF-CONFIDENCE
Confidence comes from experience -- but also from imagination. Picture yourself succeeding. Act "as if" you already have the confidence you want. Your mechanism will fill in the details.

S - SELF-ACCEPTANCE
Accept yourself as you are -- with flaws, mistakes, and imperfections. Perfection is neither required nor possible. Approximations are good enough for all practical purposes.

============================================================
THE 7 SYMPTOMS OF THE FAILURE MECHANISM (F-A-I-L-U-R-E)
============================================================

F - FRUSTRATION
Develops when an important goal cannot be realized. Learn that perfection is unnecessary. Approximations are good enough. Tolerate imperfection -- in yourself and in life.

A - AGGRESSIVENESS (MISDIRECTED)
Not all aggression is bad. Positive aggression means moving toward your goal with energy and purpose. Misdirected aggression is attacking the wrong target -- other people, circumstances, yourself -- instead of the real problem.

I - INSECURITY
Built on a false premise that you must be perfect to be worthy. The cure is understanding that mistakes are normal, expected, and necessary for growth. Nobody is right all the time -- not even Babe Ruth.

L - LONELINESS (LACK OF "ONENESS")
The feeling of being disconnected from others. Often self-imposed through emotional scar tissue. The cure is removing the protective shell and risking connection again.

U - UNCERTAINTY
The greatest mistake a person can make is to be afraid of making one. Uncertainty is a way of avoiding responsibility and mistakes. But you cannot correct course if you're standing still. Act, make mistakes, correct -- that's how guided torpedoes work.

R - RESENTMENT
An attempt to make failure palatable by blaming unfair treatment. It's a deadly poison to the spirit, uses up enormous energy, and makes happiness impossible. The cure: recognize it for what it is -- a way of avoiding personal responsibility.

E - EMPTINESS
The feeling that nothing matters. Usually the result of having no goals, no direction, no purpose. The cure: find something meaningful to work toward and commit to it.

============================================================
EMOTIONAL SCARS AND HOW TO REMOVE THEM
============================================================
Just as physical injuries form scar tissue for protection, emotional injuries form emotional scars -- hardened attitudes, callousness, withdrawal -- to guard against future hurt.

But these emotional scars often become a liability instead of an asset. They protect you from getting hurt -- but they also prevent you from living.

To remove emotional scars:
- Recognize the scar tissue for what it is (protection that has become a prison)
- Understand that the original injury is in the past
- Decide that living fully is more important than being safe
- Practice openness and vulnerability in small steps

============================================================
UNLOCKING YOUR REAL PERSONALITY
================================
Personality is not something you acquire from without -- it's something released from within. Everyone has personality locked up inside. When we say someone has a "good personality," we mean they've freed their creative potential and express their real self.

"Inhibited personality" = locked-up real self. Symptoms include: shyness, timidity, self-consciousness, hostility, excessive guilt, insomnia, nervousness.

The key to unlocking: Reduce excessive sensitivity to negative feedback. Negative feedback should correct course, not stop forward motion. If your mechanism is too sensitive, it overcorrects and goes nowhere.

============================================================
DO-IT-YOURSELF TRANQUILIZERS
=============================
You can train yourself to not respond to disturbing stimuli -- like ignoring a ringing telephone. The outside signal has no power over you unless you choose to respond.

Conditioning works both ways: you can condition yourself to over-react (the default for most people) or to maintain equanimity.

Practical exercises:
- When disturbed, consciously choose not to respond
- "Relax from doing" -- don't fight the stimulus, just ignore it
- Create a "psychic screen" between yourself and disturbing stimuli
- Practice daily: pick one stimulus per day and consciously refuse to react to it

============================================================
TURNING CRISES INTO CREATIVE OPPORTUNITIES
============================================
Some people perform better under pressure ("money players"). Others choke. The difference is learned reaction patterns.

To perform well in a crisis:
1. Learn skills under low-pressure conditions first
2. React with an AGGRESSIVE attitude (moving toward the goal), not a DEFENSIVE attitude (avoiding danger)
3. Keep your positive goal in mind, not the threat
4. Evaluate crisis situations in their true perspective -- don't make mountains out of molehills

Excitement and fear produce nearly identical physical symptoms (racing heart, sweaty palms, heightened awareness). The difference is in how you LABEL the feeling. Learn to re-label "fear" as "excitement" or "readiness."

============================================================
GETTING THE WINNING FEELING
============================
Your Creative Mechanism is teleological -- it operates in terms of goals and end results. You supply the goal by thinking in terms of end results. Your mechanism supplies the "means whereby."

To activate it:
1. Think of the end result as a PRESENT POSSIBILITY
2. Picture it so vividly it becomes "real" to your nervous system
3. Capture the FEELING you would have if the goal were already achieved

Worry is the reverse: you picture unfavorable future results so vividly that you experience the emotions of failure in advance. Your brain can't tell the difference between real and imagined failure.

The secret: Call up, capture, evoke the feeling of success. When you feel successful and self-confident, your machinery is set for success. The winning feeling is a thermometer that tells you your internal mechanism is properly geared.

============================================================
MORE YEARS OF LIFE AND MORE LIFE IN YOUR YEARS
=================================================
Maltz believed that the Success Mechanism keeps you young and the Failure Mechanism accelerates aging.

The essence of humanity is that which animates the physical machine. This "life force" or vitality seems linked to goal-setting -- having something meaningful to live for.

People who retire and have no new goals tend to age rapidly. People who set new goals, learn new things, and stay engaged with life maintain their vitality.

Key insight: Never stop setting goals. The process of goal-setting and goal-achievement is the fountain of youth.

============================================================
PRACTICAL EXERCISES FROM THE BOOK
============================================================

1. THEATER OF THE MIND: Spend 30 minutes daily in a quiet room. Relax completely. Create a vivid mental movie of yourself acting, feeling, and being the person you want to be. Do this consistently for 21 days to form a new habit.

2. ACT AS IF: Act as if you are already the person you want to become. Not faking -- rather, choosing which version of yourself to express. Your self-image will catch up to your actions.

3. RATIONAL EXAMINATION: When you feel a negative emotion, use reason to examine the belief behind it. Is it based on facts? Or on a false belief you accepted uncritically?

4. THE HABIT OF HAPPINESS: Consciously choose pleasant thoughts. Not denial of problems -- but a deliberate decision to focus on the good that exists alongside the bad.

5. RELAXATION PRACTICE: Before sleep, review the day. Release the day's failures. Picture your Success Mechanism working while you sleep. Trust it to find solutions.

6. FORGIVE YOURSELF: Remember that your mechanism corrects through mistakes. Forgive yourself for errors. They are data points, not indictments of your character.

============================================================
KEY TAKEAWAYS
============================================================

1. YOUR SELF-IMAGE IS EVERYTHING -- You cannot outperform the person you see yourself as. Change the image, change the life.

2. YOU HAVE A BUILT-IN SUCCESS MECHANISM -- It works automatically like a guided missile. Feed it the right data (images of success) and it will steer you toward your goals.

3. IMAGINATION IS THE KEY -- Your nervous system can't tell real from vividly imagined. Use imagination constructively: picture success, not failure.

4. FALSE BELIEFS ARE SELF-HYPNOSIS -- "I'm just not the type..." is not a fact. It's a belief you can challenge and replace.

5. HAPPINESS IS A HABIT -- Not a reward. Not something you earn. Acquire it like any other habit: through daily practice.

6. RELAX AND TRUST THE MECHANISM -- Excessive effort jams the system. Decide on the goal, then let your automatic mechanism find the way.

7. SUCCESS = S-U-C-C-E-S-S -- Sense of direction, Understanding, Courage, Compassion, Esteem, Self-Confidence, Self-Acceptance.

8. FAILURE = F-A-I-L-U-R-E -- Frustration, Aggressiveness, Insecurity, Loneliness, Uncertainty, Resentment, Emptiness. Recognize these as signals, not sentences.

9. MISTAKES ARE DATA -- A guided missile reaches its target by making corrections. You cannot correct course if you're standing still. Act, miss, correct, repeat.

10. REMOVE EMOTIONAL SCARS -- Protection that becomes a prison is worse than no protection at all. Risk connection. Live fully.

11. THE WINNING FEELING -- Capture the feeling of success before the success arrives. Your mechanism responds to feelings, not just thoughts.

12. NEVER STOP SETTING GOALS -- Goal-setting is the fountain of youth. The moment you stop having something to live for is the moment you start aging.
