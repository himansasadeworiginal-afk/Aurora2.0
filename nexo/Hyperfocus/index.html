Part One

Hyperfocus

The art of concentrating on one important thing at a time — your brain's most productive mode.

🧠

Attentional Space

Your mental RAM. Of 11 million bits of sensory input per second, you consciously process only 40 bits. You can hold about 4 chunks in short-term memory.

✈️

Autopilot Mode

~40% of our actions are habits. We default to autopilot, reacting to whatever is most stimulating — email pings, notifications, the news.

📊

Four Task Types

Purposeful (productive + attractive) — the sweet spot.
Necessary (productive + unattractive).
Distracting (unproductive + attractive).
Unnecessary (unproductive + unattractive).

🔄

Attention Residue

After switching tasks, part of your brain keeps processing the previous one. Task switching can make your work take 50% longer.

Part Two

Scatterfocus

Intentionally letting your mind wander — it's not slacking, it's how you plan, recharge, and connect ideas.

📝 Capture Mode

Let your mind roam freely. Capture whatever surfaces.

Best for identifying what's on your mind. Sit with a notebook and see what rises to consciousness — tasks, ideas, people to follow up with.

🧩 Problem-Crunching Mode

Hold a problem loosely while your mind wanders around it.

Best for mulling over a specific challenge. Let your subconscious make connections you wouldn't find by force.

🚿 Habitual Mode

Do a simple task while your mind wanders. The most powerful style.

Walking, showering, washing dishes. The mindless task frees your attentional space for insights. This is where eureka moments are born.

✅ Quick Tactics

Put It Into Practice

Small, actionable changes you can make today.

  • ✓
    Leave your phone in another roomSmartphones are productivity black holes. Physical distance reduces temptation.
  • ✓
    Set 3 daily intentions each morningDecide in advance what you'll focus on. Intention is the bouncer of your attentional space.
  • ✓
    Keep a distractions listCapture stray thoughts on paper so they don't hijack your attention while you work.
  • ✓
    Use an hourly awareness chimeEvery hour, check: "What's occupying my attentional space right now?"
  • ✓
    Schedule scatterfocus timeBlock 15 minutes to sit with a notebook and let your mind wander without guilt.
  • ✓
    Meditate for 5 minutes a dayThe single most validated way to expand your attentional space — proven to increase it by 30%+.
