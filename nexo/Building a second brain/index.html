🧠 What is a Second Brain?

A Second Brain is a trusted digital system for capturing, organizing, and retrieving your ideas and knowledge. It's like having an external memory that never forgets — freeing your biological brain to do what it does best: think, create, and connect.

🎯 The Big Idea

"Your mind is for having ideas, not holding them." The goal is to offload information storage to a digital system so you can focus on creativity and action.

⚡ Why It Matters

  • Stop forgetting great ideas
  • Connect insights across different domains
  • Reduce mental clutter and anxiety
  • Produce better creative work, faster
  • Turn information consumption into creation

📖 Progressive Summarization

Don't summarize everything at once. Let important ideas emerge naturally:

  • Layer 1: Saved notes (original content)
  • Layer 2: Bold the important passages
  • Layer 3: Highlight the most important
  • Layer 4: Executive summary (a few sentences)
  • Layer 5: Remix into something new

The organization system. Tap any card for more detail.

P Projects Short-term outcomes you're actively working on e.g. "Build portfolio", "Plan vacation", "Write report"
A Areas Long-term responsibilities you maintain e.g. "Health", "Finances", "Career", "Relationships"
R Resources Topics of interest you want to reference e.g. "Design notes", "Book summaries", "Recipes"
A Archives Inactive items from the other categories Old projects, past areas, outdated resources — kept for reference

🗂️ The P.A.R.A. Rule

Everything you save falls into exactly one of these four categories. When a project finishes, move it to Archives. When an area becomes inactive, archive it. This keeps your system clean and focused on the present.

🔹 1. Your Brain is for Having Ideas, Not Holding Them

Offload memory to your Second Brain. Trust the system so you can focus on connecting and creating.

🔹 2. Just-in-Time Organization

Don't over-organize upfront. Organize only when you need to find something. Let structure emerge naturally.

🔹 3. Intermediate Packets

Break work into small, reusable chunks — a paragraph, a screenshot, a bullet list. Remix them later into something bigger.

🔹 4. Divergent & Convergent Modes

Separate creation from editing. Capture freely first (divergent), then refine and organize (convergent).

🔹 5. Curated Consumption

Be intentional about what you consume. Capture only what resonates — surprising, useful, or thought-provoking.

🔹 6. Weekly Review

Spend 30 minutes each week reviewing your notes and projects. This keeps your Second Brain alive and relevant.
