================================================================================
                     BUILDING A SECOND BRAIN
              by Tiago Forte — Book Summary
================================================================================

WHAT IS A SECOND BRAIN?
A "Second Brain" is a digital system for capturing, organizing, and retrieving
ideas and information. It's a trusted external memory that helps you think
better, be more creative, and get things done without relying on your fallible
biological brain.

================================================================================
THE C.O.D.E. METHOD
================================================================================

C — CAPTURE
  Keep what resonates. Don't try to capture everything — be selective. Save
  ideas, insights, quotes, and anything that sparks your interest. Use a
  trusted capture tool (Notes, Notion, Evernote, etc.) that's always within
  reach.

O — ORGANIZE
  Save for actionability, not just for reference. Tiago's P.A.R.A. system:
    P - Projects: Short-term outcomes you're working on (active)
    A - Areas: Long-term responsibilities (health, finances, etc.)
    R - Resources: Topics of interest you want to reference later
    A - Archives: Inactive items from the other three categories

D — DISTILL
  Find the essence. Use progressive summarization:
    Layer 1: Saved notes (original)
    Layer 2: Bold passages (what's important)
    Layer 3: Highlighted passages (what's most important)
    Layer 4: Executive summary (a few sentences)
    Layer 5: Remix into something new (your own output)

E — EXPRESS
  Share your work. The ultimate goal is not just to consume information, but
  to create something with it. Don't wait for perfection — share your "inter-
  mediate packets" (small, incomplete, but useful outputs).

================================================================================
KEY PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1. YOUR BRAIN IS FOR HAVING IDEAS, NOT HOLDING THEM
   Offload memory to your Second Brain so you can focus on thinking and
   creating.

2. THE HERRINGBONE METHOD / JUST-IN-TIME ORGANIZATION
   Don't over-organize upfront. Organize only when you need to find something.

3. INTERMEDIATE PACKETS
   Break work into small, reusable chunks. A single paragraph, a screenshot,
   a bullet list — each is an "intermediate packet" you can remix later.

4. DIVERGENT & CONVERGENT MODES
   Separate the creative (divergent) phase from the editing (convergent) phase.
   Capture freely first, then organize and refine.

5. SCAFFOLDING
   Use templates and frameworks to reduce friction. Create note templates for
   common types of information.

================================================================================
THE 4 STAGES OF CREATIVITY (The Creative Process)
================================================================================

1. DIVERGENT EXPLORATION — Gather ideas broadly (Capture)
2. CONVERGENT SELECTION — Choose what matters (Organize)
3. DIVERGENT CONNECTION — Connect ideas in new ways (Distill)
4. CONVERGENT EXPRESSION — Produce output (Express)

================================================================================
PRACTICAL TIPS
================================================================================

- "Capture Criteria": Capture only what resonates — surprising, useful,
  or thought-provoking.
- "Curated Consumption": Be intentional about what you consume.
- "Project Lists": Keep a list of all current projects (no more than 10-15).
- "Weekly Review": Every week, review your notes and projects for 30 mins.
- "Slow Burns": Let ideas marinate over time instead of forcing immediate
  output.
- "Archiving is not deleting": Archive freely — you can always retrieve.
