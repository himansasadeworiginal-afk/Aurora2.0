🧠 The Triune Brain

Three brains, one purpose: survival. The key to reading people is the limbic brain — the emotional center that cannot lie.

  • Reptilian — Basic survival (breathing, heartbeat, fight-or-flight)
  • Limbic — Emotions, memory, nonverbal honesty ← THIS IS THE ONE
  • Neocortex — Rational thought, language, planning (also: lying)

⚡ The 3 F's — Limbic Survival Responses

🧊
FREEZE
Stop moving to avoid detection. Held breath, shoulders to ears, stiff posture.
🏃
FLIGHT
Move away from threat. Feet toward exit, leaning back, barriers (crossed arms, objects).
👊
FIGHT
Aggression. Chin jut, nostril flare, fist clench, space invasion.

🔑 The Golden Rule

"The body is the subconscious mind made visible." Words can deceive. The body always leaks the truth — you just have to know where to look.

Click any body area to uncover what it reveals.

🦶 Feet & Legs Most honest part of the body.
• Pointing away = want to leave
• "Happy feet" = excitement
• Interlaced around chair legs = anxiety
• Knee clasp = ready to go
🦵 Torso & Hips • Leaning in = interest
• Leaning/turning away = discomfort
• Torso away + face toward you = fake
• Arms akimbo = assertiveness
💪 Arms & Shoulders • Shoulders to ears = stress
• One-shoulder shrug = uncertainty
• Arms crossed high = barrier
• Arms crossed low = self-soothing
🤲 Hands & Fingers • Palms visible = honesty
• Steepling = confidence
• Wringing/rubbing = stress
• Thumbs up = high status
• Thumbs hidden = insecurity
👄 Face • Real smile = eyes crinkle (Duchenne)
• Fake smile = mouth only
• Lip compression = stress
• Nostril flare = anger
• Head tilt = interest/trust
👁️ Eyes • Pupil dilation = interest/arousal
• Pupil constriction = stress/dislike
• Blink increase = discomfort
• Brow flash = recognition
• Gaze aversion = processing or hiding

🫂 Pacifying Behaviors

When stressed, the limbic brain self-soothes. Spot these calming signals:

🤚Neck rubbing
👋Face touching
👅Lip licking
🤗Arm rubbing
🦵Leg rubbing
😮‍💨Deep exhale
👔Collar tug
💍Playing with objects

More pacifying = more stress. Look for clusters.

🧩 Reading the Signs

  • Confidence: Steepled fingers, thumbs out, palms visible, chin up
  • Anxiety: Fidgeting, pacifying, shallow breaths, feet wrapped around chair
  • Deception: Delayed responses, grooming gestures, covering mouth, gaze shifts
  • Aggression: Nostril flare, lip compression, chin jut, pointing, space invasion
  • Interest: Leaning in, head tilt, pupil dilation, mirroring your posture
  • Discomfort: Torso turn, barrier objects, feet toward exit, lip purse

🎭 The Truth About Smiles

A genuine smile (Duchenne) activates the eyes — crow's feet crinkle, the lower eyelids rise, the whole face lights up. A fake smile uses only the mouth. If the eyes don't change, the smile is social armor.

📋 The 5 Golden Rules of Observation

01
Establish a Baseline
Know how someone acts normally before judging. One gesture = nothing.
02
Look for Clusters
One behavior is unreliable. 2-3 congruent behaviors = a real signal.
03
Context is Everything
Cold room? Crossed arms might mean temperature, not defensiveness.
04
Watch for Sudden Change
A change in behavior = a change in emotional state. That's the truth.
05
Trust the Feet First
Feet are the most honest part. Start your observation there.

🎯 How to Practice

Watch people in public (coffee shops, airports, trains). Start from the ground up:

  1. Feet — what direction? Moving?
  2. Torso — leaning toward or away?
  3. Arms — open or barrier?
  4. Hands — visible or hidden?
  5. Face — genuine or social smile?

Do this for 2 minutes a day. Your observation skill will compound.
