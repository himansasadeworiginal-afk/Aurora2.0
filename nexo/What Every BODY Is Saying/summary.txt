================================================================================
                 WHAT EVERY BODY IS SAYING
    by Joe Navarro (with Marvin Karlins) — Book Summary
================================================================================

CORE INSIGHT:
The body never lies. Nonverbal behavior (tells, gestures, postures) reveals
people's true thoughts and feelings — often more accurately than words. Learn
to read the "limbic brain" responses that are hardwired and uncontrollable.

================================================================================
THE TRIUNE BRAIN (MacLean's Model)
================================================================================

1. REPTILIAN BRAIN (Brainstem / Oldest)
   - Controls basic survival: breathing, heart rate, fight-or-flight.
   - Works automatically, no conscious thought.

2. LIMBIC BRAIN (Mammalian / Mid-brain)
   - The center of emotions, memories, and nonverbal communication.
   - THIS IS THE KEY TO READING PEOPLE. Limbic responses are:
     • HONEST (cannot be faked for long)
     • UNIVERSAL (cross-cultural)
     • IMMEDIATE (automatic, not thought-driven)

3. NEO-CORTEX (New Brain / Outer layers)
   - Rational thinking, language, planning.
   - THIS IS THE BRAIN THAT LIES. Words can deceive; the body can't.

================================================================================
THE 3 F's — LIMBIC SURVIVAL RESPONSES
================================================================================

When the limbic brain detects a threat, it responds with one of three:

⚡ FREEZE — The oldest response. Stop moving to avoid detection.
    • Holding breath, stiffening, shoulders rising toward ears.
    • Feet pointing at an exit, or "freezing" mid-motion.

⚡ FLIGHT — Move away from the threat.
    • Turning feet/body toward an exit, leaning away, placing objects
      as barriers (a purse, coffee cup, crossed arms).

⚡ FIGHT — Aggression to remove the threat.
    • Invading space, chin jutting out, fists clenching, nostrils
      flaring, lips disappearing (pressed thin).

================================================================================
KEY BODY AREAS & WHAT THEY REVEAL
================================================================================

🦶 FEET & LEGS (Most Honest — Least Controlled)
  - Feet pointing away = desire to leave or disengage.
  - Happy feet (bouncing, wiggling) = excitement or positive anticipation.
  - Feet interlaced around chair legs = anxiety, discomfort.
  - Knee clasp / hands on knees = "I'm ready to go."

🦵 TORSO & HIPS
  - Leaning in = interest, engagement, comfort.
  - Leaning away / turning torso = discomfort, disagreement, rejection.
  - Torso tilted away while face is toward you = social smile, not genuine.
  - Arms akimbo (hands on hips) = readiness, assertiveness.

💪 ARMS & SHOULDERS
  - Shoulders rising toward ears = stress, anxiety (limbic freeze).
  - One-shoulder shrug = uncertainty, lack of commitment.
  - Arms crossed high = barrier, discomfort.
  - Arms crossed low = self-comfort / soothing.

🙌 HANDS & FINGERS
  - Palms visible = honesty, openness, submission.
  - Palms hidden / in pockets = concealment, discomfort.
  - Steepling (fingertips touching) = confidence, certainty.
  - Hand wringing / rubbing = stress or anticipation.
  - Finger pointing = aggression, dominance.
  - Thumbs up / visible = confidence, high status.
  - Thumbs hidden = insecurity, low confidence.

👄 FACE (Most Expressive — But Also Most Deceptive)
  - Genuine smile (Duchenne): crow's feet crinkle, eyes squint,
    whole face lights up. Cannot be faked.
  - Fake smile: mouth only, no eye involvement.
  - Lip compression / disappearing lips = stress, disagreement.
  - Lip pursing = distaste, disagreement, or thought.
  - Nostril flaring = anger, arousal, stress.
  - Chin jut = anger, defiance.
  - Head tilt = interest, trust, listening.

👁️ EYES
  - Pupil dilation = interest, arousal, cognitive load.
  - Pupil constriction = disinterest, stress, negative reaction.
  - Blinking rate increases = stress, discomfort.
  - Gaze aversion = discomfort, dishonesty, or processing.
  - Brow raise (brief) = recognition, surprise.
  - Furrowed brows = confusion, anger, concentration.

================================================================================
PACIFYING BEHAVIORS (Calming Signals)
================================================================================

When stressed, people self-soothe in predictable ways:
  - Neck touching / rubbing
  - Face touching (cheek, chin, forehead)
  - Lip touching or licking
  - Arm rubbing (self-hug)
  - Leg rubbing (wipe palms on thighs)
  - Deep exhale (sigh)
  - Adjusting collar or tie
  - Playing with jewelry, pens, objects

More pacifying = more stress. Watch for clusters.

================================================================================
GOLDEN RULES OF OBSERVATION
================================================================================

1. ESTABLISH A BASELINE
   Observe how someone acts normally before making judgments. One gesture
   means nothing — look for CHANGES from their baseline.

2. LOOK FOR CLUSTERS
   A single behavior is not reliable. Look for 2-3 congruent behaviors
   pointing to the same conclusion.

3. CONTEXT IS EVERYTHING
   Cold room = crossed arms may be about temperature, not defensiveness.
   Always rule out environmental causes first.

4. WATCH FOR SUDDEN CHANGE
   A change in behavior signals a change in emotional state. That's where
   the truth lies.

5. TRUST THE FEET FIRST
   Feet are the most honest part of the body. Start your observation there.
